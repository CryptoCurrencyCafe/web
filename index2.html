Project Proposal Presentations

Posted: Wed 25 March 2015

Next week, each team will present their idea for the project. You should plan to have no more than 5-8 minutes to present your idea, and then there will be some time for questions and discussion. You can use slides for your presentation (although it is fine is you want to present without slides), but should have things set up on a laptop ready to go when it is time for your team.

The schedule is below (but we won't rush the presentations Monday, so some of the ones scheduled for Monday may be postponed to Wednesday).

Monday, 30 March

Universal Coin Converter
Sam Abbate

Script Analysis in the Blockchain
Saba Eskandarian

An Analysis and Breakdown of SHA Hashing in Bitcoin [Slides]
Jeremy Gabalski

Multisignature Mobile Wallets
William Emmanuel and Kevin Hoffman

Examining Anonymous Marketplaces
Max Serpe, Peter Gregrowski

Financial Markets, Game Theory, and Self-Regulation
Merlin Zhang, Trisha Hajela

Pointcoin Reddit Tip Bot
Joey Weate, Charlie Harding

Wednesday, 1 April

PointCoin Pizza — Revamped [Slides]
Kyle Angelotti and Michelle Wang

PointList: the PointCoin Marketplace

Kickstarting PointCoin

Studying Coinbase Reserves as a means of Predicting Market Price

A Decentralized Banking Scheme for Communal Funds

Bitcoin Market Price

Class 18: Useful Proofs-of-Work

Posted: Mon 23 March 2015

Schedule

Wednesday's class will be a visit from Josh Cincinnati. You are encouraged to read some of his blog posts before his visit, including Minimalist Socialism through Cryptocurrency and Lenovo Under Sustained Man in Middle Management Attack. Josh graduated from UVa in 2007 (double-majoring in Political & Social Thought and Mathematics) and has been involved in several interesting companies since then.

Next week, each team will have an opportunity to present your project idea. The schedule will be posted separately soon.

Note: due to a bug in slideshare's updated player, ink markings no longer appear in the viewer.
If you download the slides, they are present though. Hopefully, the player will be fixed someday.
